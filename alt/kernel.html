Welcome to The Kernel systems research group

We solve performance, scalability, and efficiency problems across the systems software stack, in the context of operating systems, distributed systems, and high-performance computing. Our work is driven by emerging hardware technologies, and new classes of performance- and resource-hungry applications.

Our past research has involved high-performance virtualization and management of GPUs and other types of hardware accelerators, restructuring of systems software components in response to hardware-level parallelism on multi/manycore platforms, new methods for managing datacenters and server systems with high-performance interconnects and programmable networks, etc. However, our recent research is centered around the areas of edge computing and memory-centric computing.

Our research is supported with funding from the National Science Foundation, the U.S. Department of Energy, the Georgia Tech Center for Development and Application of Internet of Things Technologies (CDAIT), and with generous support from industry, including Cisco, Hewlett Packard Enterprise, Intel, Intercontinental Exchange, LexisNexis, VMware, and others.

Research Areas

  • Conducting experimental systems research
  • Focusing on operating systems
  • Virtualization
  • Systems software for heterogeneous many-core platforms
  • Emerging non-volatile memories
  • Large scale datacenter and cloud systems
  • High-performance communication technologies and support for novel end-user devices
  • Services
